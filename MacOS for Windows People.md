**🛡️ macOS Cheat Sheet**

**1\. The "Daily Driver" Shortcuts**

- **⌘ + Space**: **The Holy Grail.** Opens Spotlight. Search for apps, files, or do math.
- **⌘ + Tab**: Switch between open **Apps**.
- **⌘ + \` (Tilde)**: Switch between **Windows** of the _same_ app (e.g., two Word docs).
- **⌘ + Q / ⌘ + W**: **Quit** the whole app vs. **Close** just the current window.
- **⌘ + Comma ( , )**: Instant access to the **Settings** of the app you are currently in.
- **⌘ + Shift + 5**: Screen capture/recording menu (The "Snipping Tool" equivalent).
- **⌘ + Option + Esc**: **Force Quit** (The "Ctrl+Alt+Del" for frozen apps).

**2\. Mastering the Trackpad (The Mac's Secret Weapon)**

- **Right-Click**: Click or tap with **two fingers**.
- **Smart Zoom**: Double-tap with **two fingers** on a webpage or PDF to zoom in on a column.
- **App Exposé**: Swipe **down with three fingers** to see every window open for _just_ the current app.
- **Mission Control**: Swipe **up with three fingers** to see every single window you have open.
- **Show Desktop**: Spread your **thumb and three fingers** apart to clear all windows away.
- **Launchpad**: Pinch your **thumb and three fingers** together to see all your app icons.
- **Look Up**: Force click (press hard) or tap with **three fingers** on a word to see its dictionary definition.

**3\. Finder (File Explorer) Comparison**

| **Windows Action** | **macOS Action**       | **Mac Shortcut**                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Preview File**   | Tap **Spacebar**       | **Quick Look** (The best feature!) |
| **Rename File**    | Tap **Enter**          | **Return**                         |
| **Open File**      | **Cmd + O**            | **Command + O**                    |
| **Delete File**    | **Cmd + Delete**       | **Command + Backspace**            |
| **Move File**      | **Copy** then **Move** | **⌘+C**, then **⌘+Option+V**       |
| **Address Bar**    | Go to Folder           | **⌘ + Shift + G**                  |

**4\. Important Differences to Remember**

- **The Menu Bar**: It is always at the very top of your screen, not attached to the window.
- **The "Red Circle"**: Clicking the red "X" usually closes the window but **leaves the app running**. Look for the tiny black dot under the icon in the Dock.
- **Menu Bar Icons**: On the top right, hold **Command** and drag icons to rearrange them or pull them off to remove them.
- **AirDrop**: Found in Finder or the Control Center. It's the easiest way to send files to your iPhone or other Macs.

**5\. Customizing Your Experience**

If you find the Mac trackpad feels "backward," go to **System Settings > Trackpad > Scroll & Zoom** and toggle **"Natural Scrolling"** off. This will make it feel like a Windows scroll wheel.
