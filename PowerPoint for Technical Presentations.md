**Crafting the Deck: Less is More**

- The "Rule of One": One core point per slide. If a slide contains two distinct ideas, split them. Technical audiences get lost when forced to parse complex diagrams alongside bulleted lists.
- Visual Logic: Avoid generic clip art. Use technical diagrams (Flowcharts, Sequence Diagrams, or Architecture maps) created in tools like Lucidchart or Excalidraw, then export as SVGs for crisp scaling.
- Typography & Hierarchy: Use **sans-serif fonts (Inter, Montserrat, or Segoe UI)** with a minimum size of 24pt for body text. Use bold weights to draw the eye to the key takeaway immediately.
- Strategic White Space: Do not feel the need to fill the corners. Use white space to create "breathing room" for the viewer's brain.
- The "Appendix" Strategy: Never cram deep technical specs or edge-case configuration details into the main flow. Place these in an Appendix. If a question comes up, you can jump to them instantly with a hyperlink.

**Designing for Technical Precision**

- Code Snippets: Use a dark-mode theme for code. Use a **monospaced font (Fira Code or Consolas)**. Never embed screenshots of code; copy-paste the text and apply a code-block style manually for better readability.
- Maintain Consistency: Use the "Slide Master" to enforce font sizes, logo placement, and transitions. Nothing screams "amateur" louder than inconsistent alignment or sizing across 20 slides.
- Dark Mode Advantage: Presenting in a dark room? Use a dark background with light text. It reduces eye strain and makes the screen edges "disappear," keeping the focus strictly on your content.

**Presenting Like a Pro**

- The Presenter View: Always use the "Presenter View." It allows you to see your next slide and your notes while the audience sees only the current slide.
- **Master the Shortcuts: Present Alt +F5 or Ctrl + F5 Present from Current Shift + F5**
  - \`B\` or \`W\`: Toggles the screen to black or white during the presentation. Use this when you want to force the audience to stop looking at the screen and pay attention to \*you\*.
  - \`Ctrl + S\`: Opens your slide navigator to jump to any slide in the deck instantly.
  - \`Ctrl + L\`: Enables the Laser Pointer (though a physical clicker is usually better).
- Navigate with Non-Linearity: Don't just click "Next." Use "Zoom" (Insert > Zoom > Slide Zoom) to create an interactive table of contents that allows you to jump to sections based on the room's energy or the audience's technical level.
- The "Parking Lot": If a technical debate gets bogged down in the weeds, acknowledge it, say, "Let's put that in the parking lot for after the session," and move on. You control the clock, not the heckler.

Pro-Tip: Final Review - Run your deck through a "Speed Read." If you can't understand the slide's purpose in under 3 seconds, simplify it.
